Although you didn't find the page you were looking for, you found a nice easter egg that you can take as a souvenir as an SVG (HD vector image) or PNG.

Special thanks to the album Unknown Pleasures by Joy Division and its cover, originally designed by Peter Saville. The visualization on this site is a dynamic interpretation of the stacked plot of pulses from the first discovered pulsar, CP1919, originally created by Harold Craft at Arecibo in 1970. While the design famously appeared on page 111 of The Cambridge Encyclopedia of Astronomy (1977)—where it was discovered by Stephen Morris—it was first published in the January 1971 issue of Scientific American. This implementation is a contribution to the history of data visualization and is shared for educational purposes. Also, special thanks to Max Halford for the original script that I forked for this site.
